Education

In 2015, I pursued a Bachelor of Interior Architecture (Honours) at UNSW due to my passion for spatial and human centred design. I learnt how to design spaces focusing on function and form, as well as working with new and heritage listed buildings, large scale spaces, rooms, furnitures and fixtures. This degree gave me the opportunity to create personal and intimate experiences for users through theoretical research and understanding human psychology. I also learnt about a building's structural components and documentation drafting which helped to significantly reduce the gap between creative design and technicality. In 2019, I decided to further my studies by pursing a Bachelor of Computational Design also at UNSW. This course provided the stepping stone for my interest in digital and design. I witnessed first hand hw technology transforms the way we design and how it can transform everyday life as well. Computational design provided the complementary technological skills which enabled me to design efficiently and effectively. I was able to master 3D modelling software programs and web development languages during my studies and in my thesis project.

Work Experience

In 2019, I obtained a summer internship at Binyan Studios, one of Australia's largest 3D architectural visualisation firm. I was fortunate to be able to learn essential 3D modelling skills, as well as gain exposure to 3D artistry and project management. From 2017, I was working as a junior project manager at Unique Carpentry & Shopfitting, a small scale construction firm specialising in retail and carpentry work. I assisted with overseeing project contracts, as well as relevant project paperwork, material sourcing and purchasing and correspondence handling with strata managers, facilities managers, clients and material suppliers.
